TWEET THREAD — The Session Layer

---

1/6
I crawled 1,119 published Claude skills across 52 orgs and 13 domains and built an interactive map of the ecosystem.

The map: dhk.github.io/skill-map

What it shows is interesting. What it doesn't show is more interesting.

---

2/6
Every skill in the corpus does the same thing: makes Claude better at a task.

Nobody has published a skill for how to run a day.

That's the task layer vs the session layer. @simonw wrote about Skills before Anthropic shipped them. This gap predates the format.

---

3/6
Five territories with zero published skills:

- The Session Layer (continuity, rhythm, captain's log)
- Claude as Personal OS (orchestrated daily workflow)
- Skill Discovery (no registry, no versioning, the npm problem)
- Skill Evaluation (no benchmark, ships on vibes)
- Healthcare & Life Sciences (largest enterprise vertical, zero skills)

---

4/6
I've been building in the first territory for a year.

captain's-log, close-day, plan-day, reading-list-builder, daily-briefing, snapshot-create, tricorder, weekly-reset.

Started before Anthropic formalized the Skills format in October 2025. There was no category for it then. There still isn't one in the public corpus.

---

5/6
The session layer is the stickiest use case.

Task-layer skills are fungible. Session-layer infrastructure isn't. It knows your rhythm. It carries your history. It has seen your decisions.

You don't migrate away from something that has been running your day for a year.
